Fabric Defect Detection System Using Image Processing

A real-time deep learning system designed to automatically identify various fabric defects, improving quality control and efficiency in the textile industry

INTRODUCTION

Due to increasing demands for high-quality textiles, the automation of fabric defect detection is crucial. Traditional manual inspections are prone to human error, inefficiency, and high costs. A smart fabric defect detection system using YOLOv8 can provide a real-time, automated solution. This system can accurately detect and classify multiple defects, including holes, stains, thread issues, broken needles, and yarn shorts. This system not only detects defects but also creates a CSV file that contains information about the defects. Implementing such a system will significantly improve product quality, reduce operational costs, and increase production efficiency in the textile industry.

PROBLEM STATEMENT


The textile industry depends on the manual labor which is slow, incompetent, and full of error, making it very problematic to meet the fast pace requirements of modern era production. Errors like broken needle, hole, stain, thread, and yarn short usually go unchecked, result the product is full of defects, wastages of time & material, which is caused financial losses. Traditional system lack of real-time monitoring and sufficient analysis, which is caused delaying and producing the defective product.

Except data integration, it comes more difficult and challenging to improve the fabric quality strategies and enhancing the manufacturing process. Old and traditional inspection system which is depend on the human beings isn’t suitable to new fabric types patterns or customer demands. If textile industry wants to meet the modern era and customer demands, it is essential to adopt automated defect detection system.

Project Goals and Objectives


  • To create a smart system that can automatically find fabric quality, eradicating the need for manual inspection and reducing human error.
  • The system automatically detects errors like broken needle, hole, stain, thread, and yarn short objects, increasing high-quality fabric and eliminating poor quality products from reaching customers.
  • By using IoT technology, machine operators can monitor the error detection process in real-time, receiving early alerts and updates on any fault.

METHODOLOGY


  • My project’s methodology follows a structured workflow to build a smart fabric defect detection system. The first step was Data Collection, where I visited a textile industry to capture real-world images of knitted fabric. I ensured that the dataset was diverse by including various defects such as holes, stains, and broken needles, along with defect-free fabric, all captured under different lighting conditions and angles to make the model more robust.
  • Next was Data Preparation. Using LabelImg, I annotated the images by creating bounding boxes around each defect and converted them into the YOLO format. Each defect was assigned to one of five classes: Hole, Stain, Thread, Yarn Short, and Broken Needle. This annotation step was crucial as it taught the model exactly what to look for during detection.
  • The third stage involved Model Selection and Training. I selected the YOLOv8n model because of its high speed and efficiency, making it suitable for real-time defect detection without requiring powerful hardware. The dataset was divided into training, validation, and testing sets to ensure proper generalization and avoid overfitting. After rigorous training, the model achieved an impressive F1 score of 0.95 across all classes at a 0.411 confidence threshold, confirming its accuracy and reliability.
  • Finally came Deployment and Integration. I deployed the trained model in a web application using Streamlit to provide a user-friendly interface. The application is capable of analyzing both static images and live video streams from a smartphone camera. On detecting a defect, it communicates with an ESP8266 module to trigger an alarm, thereby alerting operators for immediate inspection and intervention.

CONCLUSION


In conclusion, this project successfully shows how artificial intelligence can be used to improve fabric quality inspection. By collecting real-world data, preparing it carefully, and training a YOLOv8 model, we created a system that can quickly and accurately detect fabric defects such as holes, stains, and broken needles. The final system, deployed through a Streamlit app and connected with an ESP8266 module, gives real-time alerts to operators, helping save time, reduce waste, and improve productivity. This project proves that combining AI with IoT can make textile manufacturing smarter, faster, and more reliable.
